    Coronary Heart Disease Prediction

    Problem: Cardiovascular disease is presently the leading problem of death worldwide. It is most common in the US. Every 40 seconds one person dies from cardiovascular disease. About 660,000 people in the US die from heart disease each year, that’s 1 in every 4 deaths. Diastolic Blood Pressure and Systolic Blood Pressure are also related to cardiovascular risk. Thus, a feasible and accurate prediction of heart-related diseases is very important. This research intends to pinpoint the most risk factors of heart diseases. We are going to analyse the datasets and compare the accuracy score in multiple the Machine Learning models. We will pick the best model which gives us the highest accuracy score.

    Machine Learning Models:
    1) Logistic Regression
    2) K-Nearest Neighbor
    3) Super Vector Machine
    4) Random Forest
    5) Deep Learning

    The dataset was collected from Kaggle website, from an ongoing Cardiovascular study of Framingham. The goal of the Machine Learning model is to predict whether the patient has a 10-year risk of future heart diseases. The Framingham dataset consists of 4238 patient’s records with 17 attributes. Our goal is to prepare the dataset for Machine Learning models to predict more accurate score based on user’s input of certain features. We will clean the data, analyze, and graph the data to have better understanding. We will test and train the clean data with each of Machine Learning model and pick the best model that produces high accuracy score.
